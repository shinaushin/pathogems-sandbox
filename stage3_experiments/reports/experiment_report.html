brca_pathway_mlp

Status: success  |  Started: 2026-04-27 00:16  |  Wall time: 27.4s  |  Git:

Analysis

Mean C-index 0.5064 — this underperforms the baseline by -0.0338 (0.5402).

MSigDB Hallmark covers only ~24% of the human transcriptome (4,383 unique genes across 50 pathways). At top_k=1000, roughly 240/1000 genes (24%) map to a named pathway; the remaining ~760 collapse into a single UNASSIGNED catch-all node that dominates the sparse first layer.

The UNASSIGNED node is the structural weakness of this architecture: it receives far more input connections than any individual pathway node, so its gradient signal drowns out the true pathway activations during training. This is why PathwayMLP underperforms the best flat MLP (0.6079 for brca_omics_topk2000) despite its biologically-informed first layer — the sparse structure is undermined by the dense catch-all node that short-circuits it.

Fold instability: fold 3 converge at epoch ≤ 2. The imbalanced first layer — one giant UNASSIGNED node alongside small pathway nodes — creates an uneven optimisation landscape where certain data splits immediately find a bad local minimum and stay there.

brca_pathway_mlp_kegg_scaled_init

Status: success  |  Started: 2026-04-28 04:09  |  Wall time: 33.4s  |  Git:

Analysis

Mean C-index 0.5511 — this improves on the baseline by +0.0110 (0.5402).

Ablation: pathway_scaled_init=True. After Kaiming init, each pathway node's weights are rescaled by 1/√(n_member_genes). Without this, large pathways (e.g. KEGG_RIBOSOME with ~90 genes) produce pre-activation variance ~6× higher than small pathways (~15 genes), causing BatchNorm to be driven by large pathways and leaving small pathway nodes starved of gradient signal. Scaled init equalises expected pre-activation magnitude at step 0.

This underperforms the base C2 KEGG run (0.5638) by 0.0127. Scaled init changes the effective learning rate per pathway implicitly — small pathways, now initialised at lower magnitude, may need more epochs to leave a near-zero regime than the early-stopping window allows.

Fold instability: fold 5 converge at epoch ≤ 2. Scaled init addresses variance at initialisation but not the UNASSIGNED node imbalance, which continues to dominate gradient updates in some splits.

brca_pathway_mlp_kegg_residual

Status: success  |  Started: 2026-04-28 04:21  |  Wall time: 43.4s  |  Git:

Analysis

Mean C-index 0.5782 — this improves on the baseline by +0.0380 (0.5402).

Ablation: pathway_residual=True. A dense linear projection W_skip (n_genes × n_pathways, no bias, Kaiming init) is added to the MaskedLinear output before normalisation and activation. This lets the model route signal through the pathway layer (structured, biologically interpretable) or bypass it (dense, unconstrained) — in the worst case it recovers flat-MLP-like behaviour; in the best case it augments pathway activations with direct gene contributions that the sparse mask excludes.

The skip connection delivers a +0.0144 gain over the base C2 KEGG run (0.5638). The model appears to benefit from the unconstrained path — likely routing signal from the ~1350 genes not assigned to any pathway directly into the activation space without being filtered through UNASSIGNED.

brca_pathway_mlp_kegg_layernorm

Status: success  |  Started: 2026-04-28 04:25  |  Wall time: 44.5s  |  Git:

Analysis

Mean C-index 0.5553 — this improves on the baseline by +0.0152 (0.5402).

Ablation: pathway_norm='layer'. Replaces the default BatchNorm1d after the sparse pathway projection with LayerNorm. LayerNorm normalises across the pathway dimension per sample, making it invariant to the absolute activation scale of each pathway node. BatchNorm normalises across the batch dimension per pathway node, so it is sensitive to the fact that large pathways produce higher-magnitude activations than small ones — even after Kaiming init, a single large pathway can skew the batch statistics and suppress gradient flow to smaller pathway nodes.

LayerNorm underperforms the base C2 KEGG run (0.5638, Δ-0.0085). With full-batch training, BatchNorm statistics are stable (no batch-to-batch noise), so it may actually be working better than expected for this setup.

Fold instability: fold 4 converge at epoch ≤ 2, suggesting that changing the normalisation layer alone does not resolve the underlying optimisation instability caused by the UNASSIGNED node.

brca_gene_attention

Status: success  |  Started: 2026-04-30 00:08  |  Wall time: 7584.7s  |  Git:

Analysis

Mean C-index 0.6331 — this improves on the baseline by +0.0929 (0.5402).

The transformer encoder treats each of the 500 selected genes as a sequence token. Gene i is projected to a 64-dimensional embedding (a learned per-gene identity vector plus a value projection of its expression level). 2 transformer encoder layers with 4-head self-attention then let every gene attend to every other, capturing co-activation patterns directly from the data rather than from curated pathway membership.

On this cohort the model beats the best flat MLP (0.6079) by +0.0252, suggesting the attention mechanism captures gene interactions that a plain linear projection misses.
